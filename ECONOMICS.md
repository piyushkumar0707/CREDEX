# Economics

Assume a qualified Credex customer buys discounted AI infrastructure credits with a $12,000 annual gross transaction value. If Credex keeps a 15% gross margin, one converted customer is worth about $1,800 gross profit in year one. Larger startups may be much higher, but $1,800 is a useful conservative planning number.

The GTM plan is mostly unpaid distribution. Hacker News and founder community posts cost time, not cash; if 20 hours of founder/operator time at an internal $50/hour creates 100 completed audits, the effective CAC per audit is $10. Direct replies on X or Reddit may be cheaper, around $4–$8 per completed audit, because the intent is clearer. A newsletter sponsorship would be avoided at first because paid CAC is likely too high before conversion is proven.

A rough funnel: 100 completed audits → 25 email captures → 8 high-savings leads → 3 consultations booked → 1 credit purchase. At $1,800 gross profit per purchase, that supports up to $18 effective CAC per completed audit. Since the first channels are manual and unpaid, the tool can be profitable even with low conversion if the leads are truly qualified.

To drive $1M ARR in 18 months, Credex would need roughly $6.7M in annual credit volume at a 15% take rate. At $12,000 average annual credit purchase, that is about 556 customers. If 8% of audits become consultations and 30% of consultations buy, the tool needs about 23,200 completed audits over 18 months, or about 1,290 per month. That is plausible only if the public reports and benchmark content create an organic loop beyond manual founder outreach.
